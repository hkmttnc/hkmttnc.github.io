Grace Brewster Murray Hopper

1- Life of Grace Murray Hopper

Grace Hopper was born on December 9,1906 in New York.[i] Her parents were of Dutch and Scottish descent. She started the primary school in Plainfield,New Jersey. Then , she attended to Phi Beta Kappa and graduated from there in 1928 with Mathematics and Physics degree. Hopper earned master degree Yale University in 1930. Hopper began teaching mathematics at Vassar in 1931, and was promoted to associate professor in 1941. She was married to professor Vincent Foster Hopper and they divorced in 1945. She was not married but kept her surname “Hopper”. She was an American admiral in United States Navy.. On the other hand, Grace Hopper was a valuable Computer Scientist. “Amazing Hopper” died on January 1, 1992 (aged 85) in Arlington,Virginia(US).
Figure 1 : Grace Murray Hopper (January 1984)

2- Career of Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper was a valuable computer scientist because she was one of the first developers of the Harvard Mark 1 computer in 1944 also invented the first compiler for a computer programming language. She was one of the first developers of the COBOL“Common Business-Oriented Language” which one of the first modern computer programming languages.Also she was one of the developers of UNIVAC 1 (Universal Automatic Computer 1) . Hopper was the director of the Navy Programming Languages Group in the Navy’s Offfice Information Systems Planning. She is still only computer scientist to have a Navy warship.
